# Industry research: legal

## Top commercial players

1. **Clio** — Cloud-based practice management leader with the broadest integration ecosystem (250+). Founded 2008 (Burnaby, BC). ~150,000 customers across 100+ countries. Segment: SMB | mid-market | enterprise (post-ShareDo acquisition).
2. **MyCase** — All-in-one platform optimized for solo and small firms; emphasis on client engagement, payments (LawPay), and ease of use. Founded 2010 (San Diego, CA); acquired by Apax 2020. Tens of thousands of firms (vendor does not publish exact count). Segment: SMB.
3. **PracticePanther** — Budget-friendly automation-focused PM for small firms; owned by Paradigm (formerly ASG LegalTech). Founded ~2012 (Miami, FL). Vendor claims "tens of thousands" of users; exact count undisclosed. Segment: SMB.
4. **Smokeball** — Document-heavy practice platform with deep Microsoft Word/Outlook integration and passive ("AutoTime") time capture. Founded 2010 (Chicago, IL). Vendor cites ~10,000+ firms; exact count undisclosed. Segment: SMB | lower mid-market.
5. **CARET Legal** (formerly Zola Suite) — End-to-end PM with native email client and built-in double-entry accounting; pitched at SMB/mid-market firms wanting consolidation. Founded 2016 (as Zola Suite, NY); rebranded CARET 2022. Customer count undisclosed. Segment: SMB | mid-market.

## Synthesized capabilities

### Must-have (v1)

(Capabilities present in ≥4 of 5 players. These are table-stakes.)

- **Matter / case management** — central record for a legal matter (parties, dates, status, custom fields).
- **Contact / CRM with intake forms** — lead capture, prospect tracking, conversion to matter.
- **Document management** — per-matter file store with versioning and secure sharing.
- **Document automation** — merge matter data into Word/PDF templates.
- **Manual time tracking** — timers and time entries scoped to matter/activity codes.
- **Billing & invoicing** — generate LEDES-friendly invoices, multiple fee arrangements (hourly, flat, contingency).
- **Trust accounting (IOLTA)** — segregated trust ledger with three-way reconciliation.
- **Online payments** — credit card / ACH with IOLTA-compliant payment splits.
- **Legal calendar** — matter-linked events, multi-attorney views, Outlook/Google sync.
- **Conflict checking** — search across contacts/matters/communications before opening a new matter.
- **Client portal** — secure document and message exchange with clients.
- **E-signature** — native or DocuSign-bridged signing for engagement letters and pleadings.
- **Workflow / task automation** — templated task chains triggered by matter events.
- **Reporting & analytics** — origination, realization, WIP, AR aging, productivity by timekeeper.
- **Mobile app** — at minimum: time entry, calendar, document view.
- **Role-based access control** — per-user permissions including ethical-wall scoping.
- **Two-way email integration (Outlook/Gmail)** — file emails to matter, send from matter context.

### Differentiator (v1)

(Capabilities in 1-2 players, high impact. Max 3.)

- **Passive / automatic time capture** — Smokeball's AutoTime tracks app/document activity and proposes time entries; closes the leakage gap manual timers create.
- **Rules-based court deadline engine (50-state library)** — Clio's native court rules across 2,200+ jurisdictions (others lean on LawToolBox add-on); reduces malpractice-grade calendaring risk.
- **AI matter assistant (summarize, draft, ask)** — Clio Manage AI and Smokeball Archie surface matter Q&A, draft invoices/correspondence, prioritize tasks; everyone else is still catching up.

### Skip for v1

(Niche or low-ROI for a portfolio-grade enterprise demo.)

- **Court e-filing connectors (Tyler/Odyssey/File & ServeXpress)** — jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction certification work; integration burden far exceeds demo value.
- **Embedded website builder + email marketing (Clio Grow's reach)** — marketing-stack adjacent; orthogonal to core PM demo.
- **Native double-entry general ledger** — replicates QuickBooks/Xero; QBO integration is the pragmatic v1 path.
- **Medical-records management / settlement calculator (PI-specific)** — practice-area vertical, narrow audience.
- **Built-in two-way SMS provider** — telecom compliance overhead (10DLC, TCPA) is disproportionate; defer to Twilio integration.
- **Practice-area form libraries (20k+ templates)** — content acquisition problem, not a software problem.
- **Email marketing / Google Local Services Ads** — out-of-scope channel tooling.
- **Court-rules library curation** — buy/integrate (LawToolBox) rather than build.

## Notable UX patterns observed

(Patterns that recur across multiple players — these constrain dashboard design downstream.)

- **Matter as the spine** — virtually every screen filters to a "matter" (case) context; global search, time entry, documents, billing all hang off it. Seen in: all 5.
- **Persistent global timer in the top bar** — start/stop without leaving current screen, with matter+activity-code selector. Seen in: Clio, MyCase, PracticePanther, CARET Legal.
- **Tabbed matter detail page** — Overview / Documents / Time / Billing / Notes / Communications / Tasks. Seen in: Clio, MyCase, CARET Legal, PracticePanther.
- **Kanban-style task/matter board as alternate view** — Clio, MyCase.
- **"Intake pipeline" CRM screen separate from matters** — leads convert into matters at a discrete step. Seen in: Clio (Grow), MyCase, CARET Legal.
- **Trust account UI visually segregated from operating account** — colored chips, separate ledgers, hard guardrails against cross-posting. Seen in: Clio, MyCase, PracticePanther, CARET Legal.
- **Invoice approval workflow with editable PDF preview** — draft → approve → send, with redline-friendly preview. Seen in: Clio, MyCase, CARET Legal.
- **Office add-in / sidecar (Word + Outlook)** — file-this-email and merge-this-template directly from MS Office. Seen in: Smokeball, CARET Legal, Clio.
- **"Recent matters" jump list on home dashboard** — recency-weighted, with quick-action buttons (new time entry, new doc). Seen in: all 5.
- **In-app chat support widget** — Intercom-style; reduces support load and is now table-stakes for SMB SaaS. Seen in: all 5.

## Compliance / regulatory considerations

(Be specific — named regulation + clause if known.)

- **ABA Model Rule 1.6 (Confidentiality of Information)** and **Rule 1.1 Comment [8] (technology competence)** — constrain encryption-at-rest/in-transit, vendor due diligence, breach response. Drives need for SOC 2 Type II, audit logging, and per-matter access controls.
- **ABA Model Rule 1.15 (Safekeeping Property)** — mandates segregation of client funds; trust accounting feature must enforce no-commingling, no-overdraft, and three-way reconciliation.
- **State IOLTA program rules** (e.g., NY 22 NYCRR 1300.1, CA Rule 1.15, TX Rule 1.14) — require per-client sub-ledger, monthly reconciliation, retention of statements; interest remittance to the state IOLTA fund. The exact rule varies per state — the data model must support per-jurisdiction trust account configuration.
- **ABA Model Rules 1.7 / 1.9 / 1.10 (Conflicts of Interest)** — conflict-check workflow must search across current clients, former clients, opposing parties, related entities; require attestation before opening a matter.
- **ABA Model Rule 5.3 (Responsibilities Regarding Nonlawyer Assistance)** — extends supervision obligations to cloud vendors and AI tools; argues for human-in-the-loop on AI-drafted client-facing output.
- **Attorney-client privilege & work-product doctrine** — affects telemetry, training data, and AI prompts: matter content cannot be used to train third-party foundation models without privilege-waiver risk. Constrains AI architecture (private endpoints, no-retention contracts).
- **GDPR Article 6 (lawful basis), Article 9 (special-category data), Article 15-22 (data-subject rights, esp. Art. 20 portability and Art. 17 erasure)** — applies if any EU client/data subject. Erasure conflicts with litigation hold; design must support legal-hold flag overriding deletion.
- **GDPR Article 28** — DPA required with all sub-processors; relevant for any LLM/AI vendor.
- **CCPA / CPRA** (Cal. Civ. Code §§ 1798.100 et seq.) — California consumer data rights; "Do Not Sell/Share" applies even to law firm marketing CRM data.
- **HIPAA** — applicable to PI/med-mal firms handling PHI; BAA required with the PM vendor.
- **PCI-DSS SAQ-A / SAQ-D** — applies to payment processing; trust deposits via card must respect IOLTA surcharge rules (some states prohibit passing processing fees to trust).
- **State-specific advertising rules** (e.g., FL Bar Rule 4-7, NY Rule 7.1) — affect website/CRM templates and any AI-drafted marketing output.
- **EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689)** — legal-advice AI may be classified as limited-risk; transparency obligations on AI-generated text.
- **Cross-border data residency** — Canadian PIPEDA and provincial laws (BC FIPPA, QC Law 25), UK GDPR; argues for region-pinned data residency.

## Open questions for the user

(Number them so the user can answer by index. Things the next phase synthesize-spec needs answered.)

1. **Target geography for v1?** US-only lets us hardcode IOLTA + ABA rules and ship a credible trust-accounting demo. EU-inclusive adds GDPR Art. 17/20, data-residency, and DPA tooling weight. Global posture roughly triples the compliance surface.
2. **Practice-area focus or horizontal?** A horizontal PM (Clio-style) sells breadth but ships shallow. A vertical (PI like Filevine, family law like Smokeball FamilyPro, immigration like Docketwise) ships deeper workflows for the same effort. Which positioning?
3. **Do we build a native double-entry general ledger (CARET-style) or integrate with QuickBooks Online / Xero (Clio-style)?** Native = stickier and demo-impressive; integration = 10x less surface, faster to ship, matches what most firms actually want.
4. **Court-rules engine: build, buy (LawToolBox license), or skip for v1?** Build is malpractice-grade and slow. Buy adds a per-firm cost. Skip ships faster but cedes the "litigation-ready" narrative.
5. **AI posture: assistive (drafts, summaries, search) or agentic (drafts pleadings, runs workflows)?** Affects privilege-risk surface, model-routing architecture, and how aggressively we lean on Manage-AI / Archie equivalents.
6. **E-signature: build native or embed DocuSign/Dropbox Sign?** Native lowers per-signature COGS and ships a slicker UX; embed ships in a week and inherits compliance posture.
7. **Mobile app scope for v1 — read-only (time entry + calendar + doc view) or full parity?** Drives whether we need a separate mobile codebase or can ship a PWA.
8. **Passive time capture (Smokeball AutoTime equivalent) — yes/no?** It's the single biggest differentiator in this segment but requires a desktop agent (Win/Mac), which is a meaningful platform investment.
9. **Multi-tenancy model: per-firm tenant DB, per-firm schema, or shared-schema with row-level security?** Affects backup/restore granularity, conflict-check scoping, and how we honor "right to erasure" requests.
10. **Conflict-check posture: structural attestation (checkbox in matter intake) or content-scanning (search across all matter text + emails)?** The latter materially raises infra cost (search index over privileged content) but is a marketable differentiator over PracticePanther/MyCase.
11. **Trust-account hard guardrails — block invalid postings at the API layer, or warn and let supervisor override?** Affects audit-log design and the auditor story for SOC 2.
12. **Customer size sweet spot: 1-5 attorney solos, 5-25 small firms, or 25-100 lower-mid-market?** Determines whether we lean MyCase-style simplicity, Clio-style breadth, or CARET-style consolidation.
13. **Integration breadth target for v1: 5 anchor integrations (Outlook, Word, QBO, Dropbox, LawPay) or 25+?** Clio's moat is its 250+; matching that is a multi-year program — what's the credible v1 number?
14. **Demo data: synthetic firm + 50 fake matters, or anonymized real-firm dataset?** Affects how persuasive the AI/reporting demos look without raising privilege/PII concerns.

## Answers (committed defaults for v1)

These are the answers that drive Phase 3 (synthesize-spec). They're chosen for a portfolio-grade demo — credible enough to evaluate in detail, narrow enough to actually ship.

1. **Geography**: **US-only**. Hardcoded IOLTA + ABA rules. GDPR/CCPA are documented as "ready" hooks (PII attribute, audit log, export/delete endpoints) but not certified for v1.
2. **Practice-area focus**: **Horizontal (Clio-style breadth)**. The demo's value is showing the workflow chain across personas, not vertical depth.
3. **Accounting**: **QBO two-way integration** (not native GL). Faster ship, matches what 90% of SMB firms actually want.
4. **Court rules**: **Skip for v1**. Manual deadline entries with reminder rules. Documented as the obvious next-iteration win.
5. **AI posture**: **Assistive only**. Matter Q&A, draft suggestions, summarisation. No agentic actions on client-facing artifacts. Private endpoints, no-retention contracts.
6. **E-signature**: **Embed DocuSign** via a connector. Native is months of work without portfolio payoff.
7. **Mobile**: **PWA**. Time entry + calendar + doc view + chatbot. No separate native app for v1.
8. **Passive time capture**: **No**. Desktop agent is a platform investment the demo can't justify. Documented as Smokeball's clearest differentiator, marked for future.
9. **Multi-tenancy**: **Shared-schema with row-level security via EF Core query filters**. Tenant id on every domain row. Single DB; per-tenant connection-string scoping happens in the connection-resolver layer.
10. **Conflict-check**: **Structural attestation** for v1 (matter intake form has a "conflicts checked" attestation; the search runs against contacts + matter parties + opposing parties tables only). Content-scanning of emails/docs documented as the differentiator path for v2.
11. **Trust-account guardrails**: **Hard guardrails at the API layer**. Posts that would commingle, overdraw, or violate per-jurisdiction rules are rejected with Problem Details responses. Supervisor override is a separate workflow, audit-logged, with reason capture.
12. **Customer size sweet spot**: **5-25 attorney small firms**. Sweet spot for showing multi-user RBAC + simplicity simultaneously.
13. **Integration breadth target**: **5 anchor integrations** declared in `workflow.json.connectors`: `outlook` (email), `slack` (chat), `quickbooks` (accounting), `dropbox` (storage), `docusign` (e-sig). For Foundations-epic-only ship: only `slack` is wired end-to-end as a working connector; the others are slotted with their `connector.json` placeholders.
14. **Demo data**: **Synthetic firm + 50 fake matters**, seeded via an `EFCore` `DataSeeder` running in `Development` only. No real client data, no PII concerns.
